SIMILARITIES BETWEEN HUMANS AND THE OCEAN IN COMPOSITION

Both humans and the ocean share a striking similarity: they are composed largely of water. Approximately 70% of the human body is made up of water, mirroring the fact that 70% of Earth's surface is covered by oceans.

Just as the ocean is rich in minerals essential for marine life, the human body relies on similar minerals like sodium, magnesium, and potassium for vital cellular processes. These elements circulate through both ecosystems and bodies, maintaining balance and sustaining life.

The salinity of human blood is nearly identical to the salinity of seawater, showcasing a deep evolutionary connection that binds us to the marine environment. This remarkable similarity reminds us of our shared origins and interdependence with the ocean.

Key Similarities Between Humans and Oceans

Ocean Movement and Circulation

The Gulf Stream transports about 30 million cubic meters of water per second, equivalent to 30,000 Olympic-sized swimming pools every second.

Human Circulatory System

The heart pumps approximately 7,200 liters of blood daily, ensuring nutrient and oxygen exchange throughout the body.

7,200 Liters/Day

Circulatory System Length

If laid out, the blood vessels in a human body stretch about 100,000 kilometers, enough to circle the Earth 2.5 times.
